Hydrangea , commonly named the hortensia, is a genus of more than 70 species of flowering plants native to Asia and the Americas. By far the greatest species diversity is in eastern Asia, notably China, Korea, and Japan.

  • Hydrangea arborescens
    (smooth hydrangea) eastern North America
  • Hydrangea glaucescens
    China, Myanmar and Vietnam
  • Hydrangea macrophylla
    (bigleaf hydrangea) southeast Japan, southern China
  • Hydrangea hirta
    Japan

Tulips are spring-blooming perennial herbaceous bulbiferous geophytes in the Tulipa genus. Their flowers are usually large, showy, and brightly coloured, generally red, orange, pink, yellow, or white.

  • Clusianae
    (4 species)
  • Orithyia
    (4 species)
  • Tulipa
    (52 species)
  • Eriostemones
    (16 species)

Myosotis is a genus of flowering plants in the family Boraginaceae. Myosotis alpestris is the official flower of Alaska and Dalsland, Sweden. Plants of the genus are not to be confused with Chatham Islands' forget-me-nots, which belong to the related genus Myosotidium.

  • Myosotis alpestris
    (alpine forget-me-not)
  • Myosotis arvensis
    (field forget-me-not)
  • Myosotis latifolia
    (broadleaf forget-me-not)
  • Myosotis laxa
    (tufted forget-me-not or bay forget-me-not)

Location

Hydrangea in Japan

Hydrangeas are a common sight in Japan and are a significant part of Japanese culture. They are found in gardens, parks, and temples throughout the country.

Tulip in Netherlands

Tulips are found in the Netherlands, Central Asia, and Turkey. Tulips were brought to the Netherlands from the Ottoman Empire in the 15th century.

Forget-Me-Not in Asia

The Chinese forget-me-not and the Asian forget-me-not are both flowering plants native to Asia. They are related to the common forget-me-not of temperate gardens.
